A General Overview Of Detergent Industry Worldwide in 2023

History of the Detergent Industry

The detergent industry has a long history, dating back to ancient times. The earliest recorded evidence of the production of soap-like materials dates back to around 2800 BC in ancient Babylon12. German chemical companies developed an alkyl sulfate surfactant in 1917, in response to shortages of soap ingredients during the Allied Blockade of Germany during World War I134.

In the 1930s, commercially viable routes to fatty alcohols were developed, and these new materials were converted to their sulfate esters, key ingredients in the commercially important German brand FEWA, produced by BASF, and Dreft, the U.S. brand produced by Procter & Gamble1. Such detergents were mainly used in industry until after World War II1.

By the late 1940s, new developments and the conversion of aviation fuel plants to produce tetrapropylene, used in household detergents production, caused a fast growth of domestic use1.
